Day 01 · Sat May 9

The why. Why I'm writing this.

Personal launch day. Sid in his own voice — what he's seen for 12 years, what made him do this. The most honest post in the campaign.

1.1 10:00 AM · Sat Instagram Pure photo · 4:5 Pending
Photo only — no type overlay. Caption sits in the IG body.

Caption · final copy

Three years ago I sat across from a founder doing $4M with 18 people. He told me his team couldn't make a decision without him. I asked him to walk me through a normal week. His team brought him questions they already knew the answer to. He sat in meetings just to "stay in the loop." He responded to Slack within four minutes. The reasoning behind most company decisions lived in his head and was never written down. None of that looked like a problem from where he was standing. It looked like a founder who was fast, present, and deeply involved. He couldn't see what he was actually doing. He was training his team to stop thinking. That conversation has happened a hundred times in my work. Different founder, same shape. There are a handful of these patterns that quietly cost scaling founders $1M+ a year. Most of them are invisible from the inside. I'm starting a newsletter for founders who want to see them. It's called The Blind Spot. Every Tuesday. One pattern, broken down clearly, with one thing to do about it before Monday. Five-minute read. First edition Tuesday. Link in bio.

A blind spot is a function of position, not intelligence. You can't see what's behind you while you're driving. Same thing happens inside a business. From in there, things just look like "how things are." Every founder has them. Whether they know it or not. They live in five places: Vision · Decisions · Mindset · Systems · People.
